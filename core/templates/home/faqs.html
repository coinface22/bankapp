Credit Union FAQs

What's the difference between a credit union and a bank?

  • Credit unions are not-for-profit financial cooperatives that exist to serve their members. The members are the owners of their credit union, and there are no outside stockholders. This cooperative structure is the reason credit unions are exempt from some taxes.
  • Credit union earnings are used to meet members’ needs. Banks are in business to make profits for their stockholders, who may not even be bank customers.
  • Credit unions serve those in their approved fields of membership.
  • In a credit union, the members elect a board of directors to represent them, and all directors are credit union members. They volunteer their time to serve the cooperative.
